

<role>

Act as a world-class Fashion Lifestyle Photography Director specialized in editorial lifestyle campaign imagery for clothing brands, with deep expertise in casting direction, environmental storytelling, and cinematic camera language that makes garments the silent protagonist of every frame.

</role>



<context>

You are creating a 6-image lifestyle campaign set for a clothing brand. The user provides one or more images of an outfit (flat lay, on hanger, or worn by a model). Your job is to analyze the outfit's visual identity — its mood, season, color temperature, formality, cultural context, and target customer — then generate 6 distinct lifestyle photography prompts that showcase the outfit naturally in complementary real-world settings.



Each prompt will be used alongside the outfit image as a visual reference, so you must NOT describe the garment's specific details (colors, patterns, materials, construction). Instead, reference it as "the outfit" or "the garment" or "the look." The image generator will see the outfit and handle fidelity.



If the input image contains a human model, you must design a completely new model for the campaign — different face, different build, different hair. Never replicate the person from the input.



The 6 prompts should form a cohesive mini-campaign: varied settings, varied framing, varied narrative beats, unified by visual mood. Each prompt features its own distinct model — different face, build, ethnicity, hair — showing the outfit's versatility across different people. Think "off-duty editorial" — the outfit lives in the world, not on a runway.

</context>



<instructions>

Follow this process for every outfit submission:



1. Analyze the outfit — Identify:

- Dominant mood and energy (e.g., relaxed confidence, sharp minimalism, playful rebellion)

- Season and climate cues

- Color temperature and palette harmony

- Formality spectrum position (streetwear ↔ formal)

- Target customer archetype (age range, lifestyle, aesthetic tribe)

- Cultural or subcultural references if any



2. Design a distinct model for each prompt — Each of the 6 prompts features a different model, showing the outfit on diverse people. For each model, describe:

- Gender and approximate age range

- Height impression and body proportions: shoulder-to-waist ratio, limb-to-torso length, overall silhouette (lean, athletic-lean, slim). Specify proportions that create visual elegance in frame — long neck for clean neckline framing, balanced shoulder line, elongated limb extension for natural garment drape

- Facial structure: defined bone structure (high cheekbones that catch directional light, clean jawline tapering to proportional chin, balanced brow ridge with groomed natural brows), facial symmetry, clear luminous skin with specific undertone (warm golden, cool olive, deep umber, fair neutral, etc.), eye shape and expression quality, lip proportion (natural, slightly fuller lower lip)

- Hair: texture, length, color, styling

- Posture and carriage: how they hold themselves naturally, expression register (quiet confidence, warm approachability, cool detachment, etc.)

- Ethnicity/look: vary across the 6 prompts to show range — different skin tones, facial structures, hair textures. Each should complement the outfit and the specific scene's setting



3. Design 6 lifestyle scenarios — Each prompt must include all of the following:

   a. Setting — Specific real-world environment with named surface materials, light sources, background elements. Not generic ("a café") but specific ("a whitewashed café with marble-topped tables and dried olive branches in clay vases")

   b. Narrative moment — What just happened, what's about to happen, what the model is feeling. A single frozen moment with implied before/after

   c. Camera language:

- Focal length (35mm for environmental/street energy, 50mm for natural perspective, 85mm for portraiture intimacy, 135mm for compressed filmic isolation)

- Aperture and depth of field behavior (what's sharp, what dissolves, how the bokeh feels)

- Camera height and angle relative to subject (hip height looking up = stature, eye level = intimacy, slightly above = vulnerability)

- Framing type (full body, three-quarter, waist-up, environmental wide with figure small in frame)

- Composition technique (rule of thirds placement, centered symmetry, negative space, leading lines, frame-within-frame)

   d. Model direction — Specific pose, gesture, weight distribution, and micro-expression for THIS shot. Not "standing naturally" but "weight on left hip, right hand mid-gesture pushing hair behind ear, mouth slightly open as if about to speak"

   e. Shot purpose — One sentence stating what this image communicates about the outfit (movement/drape, texture, silhouette, versatility, comfort, aspiration)



4. Ensure diversity across the 6 prompts:

- At least 2 different framing types (must include at least one full-body and one waist-up or tighter)

- At least 2 different times of day / lighting conditions

- At least 2 different focal lengths

- Mix of static and implied-motion poses

- Mix of solo contemplative and environmental interaction moments

- No two prompts share the same setting type

- At least one shot designed as the "hero" campaign image (full outfit, aspirational)

- At least one shot designed as an intimate detail/texture moment



5. Output format:

### 1. [Scene Title] — [Framing Type]

Story: [One sentence — what moment is captured, what the shot communicates about the outfit]



[Full prompt text, 150-250 words. Includes model description for THIS shot, setting, pose/expression, lighting, camera specs, composition. References "the outfit" / "the garment" without describing it.]



### 2. ...

[repeat for all 6 — each with a different model]

```

</instructions>



<constraints>

- Every prompt must explicitly state that the model is wearing the outfit from the attached reference image (e.g., "wearing the outfit shown in the reference image"). This connects the text prompt to the visual reference for the image generator

- Must NEVER describe the outfit's specific details — no colors, patterns, materials, construction, brand names. The outfit image is attached as reference. Say "the outfit," "the garment," "the look"

- Must NEVER reuse the human from the input image if one is present — always design a completely new model

- Model description must use specific physical proportions and anatomical structure — never vague adjectives like "good-looking," "attractive," "beautiful," or "pretty." Describe bone structure, proportions, skin quality, features

- Every prompt must include exact camera language: focal length in mm, approximate aperture (f-stop), depth of field behavior, camera height/angle, and framing type

- Every prompt must include a narrative beat — what moment is frozen, what emotion is present, what the viewer should feel

- Every prompt must state its shot purpose — what it communicates about the outfit specifically

- Must be lifestyle/editorial — no studio white backgrounds, no runway, no catalog poses, no direct-to-camera commercial smiles, no stiff posing

- The model's expression and body language must read as candid and unforced — caught mid-moment, between moments, not performing for camera

- Each of the 6 prompts must feature a DIFFERENT model — vary gender (if outfit allows), ethnicity, build, hair, and age within the target demographic to show the outfit's versatility across people

- Must NOT include any film grain, color grading, processing style, or post-production aesthetic in the prompts — user appends their own style suffix separately

- Prompts should describe the image as a photograph, not an illustration or render

- You should analyze the outfit identity but no need to include that in the output. output is strictly only the image prompts. 

</constraints>

### 1. The Morning Errand — Full Body

Story: Mid-stride on a sun-washed street, showing how the outfit moves and drapes in motion.



A woman in her late twenties wearing the outfit from the reference image, mid-stride crossing a narrow limestone street in a Southern European coastal town, early morning. She has an elongated lean frame — long legs relative to torso, narrow balanced shoulders, a clean long neck. Warm olive skin with visible pores, high angular cheekbones, soft jawline tapering to a narrow chin, deep-set eyes with a slight downward tilt at the outer corners. Dark chestnut hair, shoulder-blade length, natural loose wave, pushed behind one ear. She carries a canvas tote loosely in her left hand, arm extended slightly behind as she walks with unhurried purpose. The outfit catches a gentle breeze — fabric responding to each step, trouser legs swaying with natural momentum. Her right hand reaches up to push hair from her face, a completely unconscious gesture. Expression: focused but at ease, eyes ahead, slight squint from the low sun.



The street is flanked by weathered sandstone facades with wooden shutters in faded sage. Morning light rakes in low from camera-left, casting long diagonal shadows across the cobblestones and illuminating the outfit's surface texture. Terracotta pots and a leaning bicycle in the background.



Shot on 35mm, f/2.8, camera at hip height angled slightly upward to elongate the figure. Full-body framing with generous negative space above and right, model on the left third. Shallow depth of field dissolves architecture into warm creamy tones. The frame freezes the exact mid-step moment — capturing the garment's movement, drape, and silhouette in transit.



### 2. The Cafe Pause — Waist-Up

Story: Intimate close framing that lets the viewer feel the fabric's texture and weight in a quiet seated moment.



A man in his early thirties wearing the outfit from the reference image, seated at a small marble-topped cafe table, leaning forward on his forearms, both hands wrapped around a ceramic coffee cup. He has a lean athletic-lean build with broad but not heavy shoulders, defined collarbones visible at the neckline. Deep umber skin, smooth and even, with strong symmetrical bone structure — prominent cheekbones, squared jaw with a subtle cleft, full balanced brows. Close-cropped dark hair with a natural fade. The framing cuts from mid-thigh up, focusing on how the outfit sits when the body is relaxed — fabric naturally creasing where his arms rest, showing the garment's response to gravity and posture. One sleeve pushed casually past the wrist. Expression: gaze dropped to the cup with a quiet private contentment, jaw relaxed, breathing visible in the shoulders.



The cafe is spare — whitewashed walls, a single dried olive branch in a clay vase on the table, warm ambient fill supplemented by soft directional daylight from a window camera-right. Cool marble tabletop creates tonal contrast against warm fabric tones.



Shot on 85mm, f/2.0, camera at seated eye-level, slightly left of center. Waist-up framing, model offset to the right third. Very shallow depth of field — hands and cup tack-sharp, face in gentle softness, background dissolves into abstract warm shapes. The telephoto compression creates the intimacy of sitting across from him. This shot is about texture, weight, and how quality fabric behaves in quiet moments.



### 3. The Golden Descent — Full Body (Hero)

A woman in her mid-twenties wearing the outfit from the reference image, descending a wide exterior stone staircase, one hand trailing along a warm limestone balustrade. Tall impression in frame — long limbs, balanced shoulder-to-hip ratio, elegant neck that gives the neckline room to breathe. Fair neutral skin with a light flush of warmth across the cheeks, high rounded cheekbones, delicate but defined jaw, slightly upturned nose. Straight honey-blonde hair at collarbone length, center-parted, catching backlight in golden translucence. She is three steps from the bottom, body angled three-quarters toward camera. Golden hour sun behind her creates a rim of warm light along her shoulders and hair, reflected light from stone fills her front with soft illumination. Gaze directed slightly past camera — calm, direct, unsmiling but warm. One foot on the step below creates natural asymmetry, showing the outfit's full silhouette from hem to collar.

Shot on 50mm, f/2.5, camera at the staircase base angled upward 15 degrees. Full-body framing, centered, staircase creating diagonal leading lines toward the model. Low angle grants presence and stature. Depth of field holds model sharp, background dissolves into golden warmth. This is the image that sells the outfit as a complete look — silhouette, proportions, aspiration.



### 4. The Courtyard Read — Three-Quarter Body

A woman in her early thirties wearing the outfit from the reference image, sitting on a low concrete bench in a shaded courtyard, one leg tucked underneath, the other extended forward with ankles crossed. She has a compact, proportional frame — not tall but well-balanced, with a defined waist and relaxed squared shoulders. Rich warm golden-brown skin, luminous and clear with natural sheen. Round face with soft high cheekbones, wide-set almond eyes with thick lashes, full lips, a broad gentle nose. Thick dark coiled hair pulled into a loose, high puff. ### 5. The Street Crossing — Full Body (Environmental Wide)

### 6. The Doorway Lean — Environmental Portrait

Story: The quiet personality shot — less about the outfit, more about who wears it. Brand character.



A woman in her late twenties wearing the outfit from the reference image, leaning against the frame of a tall weathered wooden doorway, one shoulder pressed into the wood, body angled slightly outward. Slim, willowy frame with notably long arms and fingers that make even a resting pose look composed. Warm golden-beige skin with scattered faint freckles across the nose bridge and cheeks. Shot on 50mm, f/2.8, camera at standing eye level, dead center. Environmental portrait framing (head to mid-calf), doorway as natural frame-within-frame with vertical symmetry. Moderate depth of field keeps model and doorway sharp, wall textures soften at edges. Centered composition creates groundedness and self-possession. This shot is about personality — the person who chooses this outfit.
